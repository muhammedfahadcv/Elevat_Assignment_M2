Front-End Interface Developers

There are two primary roles in front-end development: Front-End Interface Developers and . While they might sound similar, several distinctions lie between the two.

Front-End Engineers

Front-End Interface Developers (or Front-End Designers) take drawings and other static images and translate them into web pages. This is a big task, as the web pages need to match the designs and ensure they work on various screen sizes. Front-End Engineers are less focused on design and more on making the website functional and scaleable. Some Front-End Engineers also do design work, and some workplaces use the two job titles interchangeably.
